About Me

My name is Maggie Woodard. I am from 38 years old. I was working for a local criminal defense attorney when I decided to enroll in the Mississippi Coding Academies. I have a friend who went through the academy and thought it may be something I would be interested in trying. I gave it some thought for about two weeks and one day I decided to just go for it... So here I am.

I love to paint, listening to music, and traveling. Most of all, I love being a mother.I have a son who is eleven years old and two daughters ages nine and seven. My son plays baseball and both my daughters play softball. We spend most of our weekends at the fields. I grew up playing softball so I am thrilled that my kids have a love for the game. I also enjoy painting, mostly abstract paintings. I love listening to music, it soothes my soul. Some of my favorite musicians are the Grateful Dead, Stevie Nicks, Bob Dylan and the Doors just to name a few. I love to travel. Before I had children I lived in Colorado, Memphis, and Arkansas. Colorado was my favorite. Being in the mountains is so peaceful and serene. It was there that I learned to meditate. Meditation is a huge part of my life.

I am passionate about helping others and advocate for men and women in recovery. I work for Oxford House of Mississippi and have been affiliated with them for over two years. Oxford Houses are self-run, self-supporting addiction recovery homes for individuals who have stopped using alcohol and drugs, hoping for a second chance in life. The first Oxford House opened in 1975 and has since grown to over 1900 homes world-wide. All of our houses are gender specific, which includes houses for men, women, and women with children. Safe, structured, and supportive environments help to create an atmosphere of growth, responsibility, and self-efficacy.
